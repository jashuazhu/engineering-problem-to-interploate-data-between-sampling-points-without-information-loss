Equivalent Digital domain Ideal reconstruction

Assume a real, baseband, bandlimited signal x(t) with one-sided bandwidth B, sampled at fs>2B. Let samples be x[n]=x(nTs) with Ts=1/fs​.

Whittaker–Shannon (cardinal series):

\[ { \;\hat x(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x[n]\;{sinc}\!\Big(\frac{t-nT_s}{T_s}\Big)\; } \quad\text{with}\quad {sinc}(u)=\frac{\sin(\pi u)}{\pi u} \]

This equals the original x(t)if x is strictly bandlimited to ∣f∣≤B

So the “interpolation filter” is just the ideal brick-wall LPF that removes spectral images at ±k*fs
