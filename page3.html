Equivalent Digital domain Ideal reconstruction

Index Page 1 Page 2 Page 3

Assume a real, baseband, bandlimited signal x(t) with one-sided bandwidth B, sampled at fs > 2B. Let samples be x[n] = x(nT_s) with T_s = 1/fs.

Whittaker–Shannon (cardinal series):

$$ \hat x(t)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x[n]\,\operatorname{sinc}\!\Big(\tfrac{t-nT_s}{T_s}\Big),\quad \operatorname{sinc}(u)=\frac{\sin(\pi u)}{\pi u} $$

This equals the original x(t) if x is strictly bandlimited to |f| \le B < fs/2. In frequency, the interpolation filter is the ideal brick-wall LPF that removes spectral images at ±k·fs.
