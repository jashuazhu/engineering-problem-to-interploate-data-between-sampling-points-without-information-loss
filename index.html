Nyquist Recovery Demonstration

Analog domain and Digital domain

Problems definition: Signal processing challenges frequently arise in engineering applications. For instance, detecting a pulse in time-series data is essential in various fields—such as identifying an echo pulse from a medical ultrasonic transducer or recognizing an anomalous pattern from a spaceborne sensor on earth orbit. In these cases, it is critical to accurately determine the exact peaks and valleys of the signal and report their time of occurrence. To enable advanced mathematical analysis, signals are often converted into the digital domain, which requires sampling of the underlying analog data. Ideally, we want to minimize the sampling rate to reduce computational effort, while still preserving all the necessary information. This principle is governed by the Nyquist Sampling Theorem.
