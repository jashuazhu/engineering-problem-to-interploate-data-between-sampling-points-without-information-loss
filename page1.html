Experiment: The signals

Time-domain views of analog reference, discrete samples, and zero-padded sequence.

In this study, we demonstrate signal recovery using three representative examples:.

  • Sinusoidal Wave: A sinusoidal signal with frequency B/2B/2B/2 and a phase shift. The objective is to determine whether the original amplitude and phase can be accurately recovered.
    • \[ f_{\text{sin}}(t) = A \cos\!\left(\frac{B}{2}t + \phi \right)fsin(t)=Acos(2Bt+\phi ) \]
  • Gaussian Pulse: A Gaussian-shaped pulse with an effective bandwidth of B/2B/2B/2. Here, we investigate whether the correct peak value can be recovered when the sampling instants do not coincide with the pulse center.
    • \[ f_{\text{gauss}}(t) = A \, e^{-\frac{t^2}{2\sigma^2}}, \quad \text{with } \sigma \sim \frac{1}{B} \]
  • Band-Limited Rectangular Pulse: A rectangular pulse that has been band-limited by an ideal low-pass filter (LPF). We again study whether the correct peak value can be reconstructed when the sampling instants are offset from the true maximum.
    • \[ f_{\text{rect}}(t) = A \, \frac{\sin(\pi B t)}{\pi t} = A \, \text{sinc}(B t) \]

    For each of these signals, three different representations are examined:

  • f_analog: The analog reference curve. Since an ideal continuous waveform cannot be plotted directly, we approximate it by oversampling at a very fine rate fs.
  • f_digital: The discretely sampled curve obtained at the critical rate 2B, may miss the peak or valley of analog signal.
  • ef_pad: A zero-padded version of the digital curve, where additional samples are inserted at the fs, This allows us to illustrate the replicated spectra beyond the band ±B.

    • Time Domain

    - s_fine approximates the analog curve on a very fine grid (fs_fine).
    - s_samp are samples at fs ≈ 2B; markers show discrete points.
    - s_zero places sample values onto the fine grid at sample instants.
