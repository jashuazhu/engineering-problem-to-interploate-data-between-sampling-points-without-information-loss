Experiment: The signals

Time-domain views of analog reference, discrete samples, and zero-padded sequence.

In this study, we demonstrate signal recovery using three representative examples:

  • Sinusoidal Wave: A sinusoidal signal with frequency B/2 and a phase shift. The objective is to determine whether the original amplitude and phase can be accurately recovered.
    • $$ f_{\text{sin}}(t) = A \cos\!\left(\tfrac{B}{2} t + \phi \right) $$
  • Gaussian Pulse: A Gaussian-shaped pulse with an effective bandwidth of B/2. Here, we investigate whether the correct peak value can be recovered when the sampling instants do not coincide with the pulse center.
    • $$ f_{\text{gauss}}(t) = A \, e^{-\tfrac{t^2}{2\sigma^2}},\quad \sigma \sim \tfrac{1}{B} $$
  • Band-Limited Rectangular Pulse: A rectangular pulse that has been band-limited by an ideal low-pass filter (LPF). We again study whether the correct peak value can be reconstructed when the sampling instants are offset from the true maximum.
    • $$ f_{\text{rect}}(t) = A \, \frac{\sin(\pi B t)}{\pi t} = A\,\operatorname{sinc}(B t) $$

    For each of these signals, three different representations are examined:

  • f_analog: The analog reference curve. Since an ideal continuous waveform cannot be plotted directly, we approximate it by oversampling at a very fine rate fs.
  • f_digital: The discretely sampled curve obtained at the critical rate 2B; markers show discrete points and may miss the analog peak/valley.
  • f_pad: A zero-padded version of the digital curve, where additional samples are inserted at fs to illustrate the replicated spectra beyond ±B.

    • Time Domain

    - s_fine approximates the analog curve on a very fine grid (fs_fine).
    - s_samp are samples at fs = 2B; markers show discrete points.
    - s_zero places sample values onto the fine grid at sample instants.
